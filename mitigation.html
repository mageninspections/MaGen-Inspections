Radon Mitigation

A professionally installed mitigation system will reduce radon levels in your home by up to 99% — permanently.

What Is Mitigation?

How We Remove Radon From Your Home

Radon mitigation uses sub-slab depressurization (SSD) — a vent pipe and fan installed beneath your slab that draws radon from the soil and routes it safely above the roofline before it can enter your living space.

Most systems reduce radon by 90–99% and are installed in a single day.

Sub-slab depressurization system diagram

System Components

What Makes Up a Mitigation System

Each installation is designed specifically for your home's foundation type and soil conditions.

⬇

Suction Point

A hole is drilled through your concrete slab, creating direct access to the soil and aggregate beneath. This is where radon is captured before it enters your living space.

↑

PVC Vent Pipe

A sealed pipe routes captured radon from the suction point, up through or along the exterior of your home, and terminates safely above the roofline — well away from windows and doors.

⊕

Inline Fan Unit

A continuously running, energy-efficient fan maintains constant negative pressure beneath the slab. Most fans are nearly silent and consume minimal electricity — similar to a light bulb.

⛨

Sealed Entry Points

Visible cracks, gaps around pipes, and sump openings are sealed with appropriate materials to prevent radon from bypassing the system and entering through secondary paths.

◉

System Monitor

A visual pressure indicator (U-tube manometer) is installed on the pipe so you — and future home inspectors — can confirm the system is functioning correctly at a glance.

✓

Post-Install Test

After installation, we place a test device to verify actual radon reduction. We confirm results meet EPA guidelines before the job is considered complete.

Installation Day

What to Expect During Installation

Most installations are completed in a single day with minimal disruption to your household.

1

Site Assessment (30–45 min)

We walk through the basement or crawlspace to determine the optimal suction point location, pipe routing path, and fan placement.

2

Core Drilling & Sealing (1 hr)

We drill a 4" core through the slab at the determined location, seal cracks and gaps, and prepare the pipe routing path.

3

Pipe & Fan Installation (1–2 hrs)

The PVC vent pipe is installed through or along the exterior wall and routed to above the roofline. The fan unit is secured and connected.

4

System Check & Test Placement (30 min)

We confirm the system is operating correctly using a manometer, then place a post-installation radon test device. We review everything with you before leaving.
