Why Ohio Matters

Ohio Is a High-Risk Radon State

The U.S. EPA divides the country into radon risk zones. Ohio is predominantly classified as Zone 1 — the highest-risk category, meaning average indoor radon levels are predicted to exceed 4 pCi/L.

Ohio's geology — particularly its glacial deposits and underlying shale formations — makes it especially susceptible to elevated radon. Many Columbus-area homes have been found to have radon levels well above the EPA action threshold.

⚠
Don't assume your home is safe

Radon levels vary dramatically from house to house, even on the same street. The only reliable way to know your level is to test.
